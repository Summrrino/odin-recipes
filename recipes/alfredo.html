Chicken Alfredo

Description

Chicken Alfredo is a classic Italian dish that combines tender, pan-seared chicken with creamy and indulgent Alfredo sauce, served over long strands of fettuccine pasta. The Alfredo sauce, made from butter, minced garlic, nutmeg, heavy cream, and Parmesan cheese, is reduced on the stovetop until it achieves a creamy consistency that perfectly clings to the pasta. This dish is a dream-team combination of juicy, golden-brown slices of chicken breast and slurp-able strands of thick fettuccine pasta. It’s a rich and satisfying meal that’s sure to impress. Enjoy a bowl of Chicken Alfredo for a taste of authentic Italian cuisine right at home.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound uncooked fettuccine or linguine
  • 2 boneless chicken breast halves, cooked and cubed
  • 1 (16 ounce) jar Alfredo-style pasta sauce
  • 1 (10 ounce) package frozen mixed vegetables
  • 1 (4.5 ounce) can sliced mushrooms
  • ⅓ cup milk

Steps

  1. Fill a large pot with lightly salted water and bring to a rolling boil. Cook fettuccine at a boil until tender yet firm to the bite, about 8 minutes. Drain well.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, place cubed cooked chicken, Alfredo sauce, frozen vegetables, mushrooms, and milk in a large saucepan over medium-low heat. Cook and stir until chicken is heated through and vegetables are tender.
  3. Serve warm Alfredo and chicken sauce over cooked noodles.
